The End & Beginning

Epilogue

“He was ill, but he was not dead. He lay on his cot, looked at the sky, and for the first time in years, he did not feel alone.”

Raskolnikov is sentenced to eight years of hard labour in Siberia. Sonia follows him. In prison, surrounded by illness and isolation, the transformation Raskolnikov resisted for the entire novel finally begins — not through punishment, but through love. He picks up the Bible. He weeps.

Dostoevsky does not wrap this neatly. The redemption is a beginning, not an end. After 500 pages of psychological torment, hope arrives not with a flourish, but with a sunrise.

REDEMPTION IS NOT GIVEN. IT IS EARNED THROUGH SUFFERING.
