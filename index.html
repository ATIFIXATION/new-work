Screenwriting Fundamentals

Setup &
Payoff

Setup

Must be established at the beginning of the story — plant it early so the audience registers it without knowing why.

Payoff

The moment the setup pays back — the gun shown must be fired. The chamber checked must matter.

→

Our brain always connects things — that's why setup & payoff works. The mind hunts for patterns and rewards them with satisfaction.

Rule 02

Start Late. Leave Early.

Never show the whole conversation between two characters. Show only the highlighting factor — the moment that actually moves the story forward.

EG: Character A says "Holidays — two months!" No filler. No small talk.

DRAMA
The Craft of Storytelling

How to Write
Drama.

01

Finding Answers

Create a mystery or problem so your audience must be intrigued to find it. Your character is either finding something — or trying to achieve something. Both create forward pull.

02

Order & Chaos

Life flows: Order → Chaos → Order. Give your character a constant life, then chaos disrupts it, then order returns — transformed. The disruption IS the story.

03

Character Exposition

Some scenes feel irrelevant — but keep them if they reveal character nature. A scene showing a "helping" nature has relevance even if the plot doesn't need it.

Narrative Journey

Every story has an Internal Journey (regret, self-evaluation) and an External Journey flowing through your scenes.

Character Depth

Show traits — Beauty, Loyalty. Remove good traits to create less likeable but far more interesting characters.

Exposition Hook

Father's health issues → audience is now intrigued whether the character achieves their goal or not.

The Writer's Manifesto

Master Rules
of the Craft

#

Every scene must be building towards the next scene.

#

Be a conductor of audience emotions — bring contradictory emotions out of them.

#

Write your screenplay for the reader — it must be felt on the page.

#

Be true to your characters. Let them make decisions you wouldn't.

#

Have a vision. Make it authentic — then have the guts to share it.

#

Write your characters first. Story must constantly unfold from them.

#

Treat suspense like a rubber band — stretch it, don't snap it too soon.

#

Give characters decisions & consequences → Moral Choices.

#

Put clues — let your audience decide for themselves.

#

Rule out morality to create interesting characters — complexity over virtue.
